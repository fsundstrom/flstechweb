My Arduino for R1D1 Rover

I have the Raspberry Pi running Linux with a WiFi adapter and web server.  The Pi is the commander getting instructions a Perl CGI script and putting them in a queue that gets processed by another Perl script that decides what needs to be done and sends commands to the Ardunio via a USB connection.  They now sell a Pi with the Arduino on one board.

Then ardunio just executes simple instructions like move forward and sends back data like how many clicks the wheels have moved.  This keeps the Arduino code fast and simple.
  
The Ardunio has 6 ultrasonic sensors and 2 bumpers to detect objects, a compass to stay on a heading, IR sensors on the gears for distance and simple LEDs and buttons.  The Pi has a GPS Sound system and keeps track of the whole picture.  



Motor controller

I used the Pololu Dual MC33926 Motor driver shield for my rover.  This can handle up to 5A per motor and has circuit protection along with Arduino libs.  This worked great with the UNO but not with the mega 2560. Arduino had changed the time used to timer2 an 8 bit timer for pins 9 and 10 not timer1 the 16bit timer used on the UNO and the motors were loud and did not have the same power as the modulation was at a lower frequency.
